Healthcare Robot 'Moxi' Saves Clinical Staff Over 1.5 Billion Steps

April 18, 2025 By Healthcare Team

Diligent Robotics' Moxi, a mobile manipulation robot designed specifically for healthcare environments, has reached a significant milestone in assisting medical professionals. According to data released by the company, Moxi robots have collectively saved clinical staff over 1.5 billion steps by handling routine logistics tasks that would otherwise require nurses and other personnel to traverse hospital corridors multiple times per shift.

Since its commercial launch in 2020, Moxi has been deployed in hundreds of hospitals across North America and Europe. The robot's ability to autonomously handle routine logistics tasks has dramatically improved workflow efficiency and allowed clinical staff to focus more on direct patient care.

"When we first started developing Moxi, we knew that nurses were spending roughly 30% of their time on tasks that didn't require their clinical expertise," explains Dr. Andrea Thomaz, co-founder and CEO of Diligent Robotics. "What we didn't fully appreciate was how physically demanding those tasks were. Each mile we save a nurse from walking is another mile they can dedicate to patient care instead of logistics."

Beyond Step Counting: The Real Impact

While the 1.5 billion step milestone is impressive, hospital administrators report that Moxi's impact extends far beyond simply reducing walking distances. Nurse satisfaction scores have increased by an average of 22% at facilities where Moxi has been deployed for more than six months, and staff retention rates have improved by 18% compared to pre-deployment figures.

Northwestern Memorial Hospital in Chicago, which has been using Moxi robots since 2022, reports that the time nurses spend on direct patient care has increased from 56% to 71% of their shifts. This significant improvement has contributed to better patient outcomes, with reduced call button response times and higher patient satisfaction ratings.

Technical Evolution

The current generation of Moxi robots represents a significant advancement over the original prototype. Key improvements include:

  • Enhanced navigation capabilities that allow the robot to operate effectively in crowded corridors and adapt to unexpected obstacles
  • Expanded manipulation skills that enable Moxi to interact with a wider range of hospital equipment and supplies
  • Improved social intelligence features that help the robot communicate effectively with staff and patients
  • Integration with hospital systems including electronic health records, material management, and staff communication platforms
  • Remote monitoring and support capabilities that allow for rapid troubleshooting and software updates

Particularly noteworthy is Moxi's ability to adapt its schedule based on real-time need. The robot can prioritize urgent tasks, such as delivering critical medications or supplies, while postponing less time-sensitive errands. This dynamic scheduling has proven especially valuable during high-demand periods such as shift changes or emergency situations.

Staff Acceptance and Integration

Initially, some healthcare facilities reported challenges with staff acceptance of robot assistants. However, Diligent Robotics has refined its deployment approach to focus on gradual integration and emphasizing the robot's role as an assistant rather than a replacement for human workers.

"The key to successful deployment has been our 'helper, not replacement' messaging," notes Sarah Johnson, Chief Nursing Officer at Mercy Hospital in St. Louis. "Once our staff understood that Moxi was there to take on the tasks that pulled them away from patients, resistance quickly turned to enthusiasm. Now, our nurses actively look for new ways to utilize Moxi's capabilities."

This shift in perception is reflected in how staff interact with the robots. Many hospitals report that their Moxi units have become unofficial team members, with staff customizing the robots with seasonal decorations and even including them in team photos.

Future Developments

Building on this success, Diligent Robotics is developing expanded capabilities for the next generation of Moxi robots. These include more advanced manipulation skills for handling an increased range of medical supplies, enhanced communication interfaces for better interaction with staff and patients, and adaptable learning algorithms that allow the robot to optimize its routes and procedures based on facility-specific patterns.

"We're just scratching the surface of what robotics can do to support healthcare," says Dr. Thomaz. "Our vision is to create robotic systems that seamlessly integrate into healthcare environments and take on more of the routine tasks, allowing clinical staff to focus their expertise where it matters most: providing compassionate, high-quality care to patients."

With healthcare facilities worldwide continuing to face staffing challenges and increasing demand for services, solutions like Moxi represent an important development in the ongoing effort to optimize clinical workflows and improve both staff and patient experiences.
